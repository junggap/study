Grammar Corrections Matrix
What You Said Recommended Correction Grammar Rule / Explanation
...I've just been in the office. ...I've just got to / arrived at the office. "Been in" implies you were there and left. To say you recently arrived, use "got to" or "arrived at."
...free of works... ...free from work / work is a bit slow... "Work" is uncountable here. Use "free from work" or describe the day as "slow."
...the project is almost at the end... ...the project is wrapping up / nearing completion... More idiomatic and dynamic industry phrasing for finishing projects.
work will go slowly things will be pretty slow today Native speakers describe the day or workflow environment as slow, rather than the work itself.
Temporaly Temporarily Spelling correction (pronounced tem-puh-rer-uh-lee).
...the other team's project... ...another team's project... Use "another" when introducing a non-specific team or topic for the first time.
...get back to home team... ...go back to my home team / core team... Requires a clear possessive pronoun ("my").
...the another project... ...another project / a new project... "Another" already means "an other." Never precede it with the article "the."
...catch up all that I missed. ...catch up on everything I missed. The standard transitive phrasal verb requires a preposition: "catch up on [something]."
...now a days... ...nowadays... Must always be written together as a single compound word.
...study with AI... ...use AI to catch up / leverage AI... "Use AI" or "leverage AI" sounds significantly more professional than "studying with" a tool.
I ask them to AI... I ask the AI / I ask AI... Direct object structure: you "ask the AI [a question]" or simply "ask AI."
...how i getting to know. ...how I am learning / processing it. Requires a continuous auxiliary verb ("I am") and a natural verb choice for professional context.
Amount of HTML files Number / Volume of HTML files Use "number of" for countable plural items (files). Reserve "amount of" for uncountable abstract elements.
the sidebar is enough a sidebar is enough Use the indefinite article "a" when referring to a general design option or component.
This workflow is not an option. This workflow is a must-have / mandatory. "Not an option" can accidentally sound like "impossible." To clarify necessity, use "a must-have."
