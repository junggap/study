Key Vocabulary & Grammar Tips

  • Opened vs. Released/Dropped: When a streaming service makes something available, we say they released it, added it, or colloquially, 'dropped' it.
  • Spelling: Remember that 'logged' has two gs (log → logged), and 'announced' has two ns.

Key Vocabulary & Grammar Tips
  • Unconciently vs. Subconsciously: 'Unconciently' isn't a word in English. If you mean something happening beneath your active awareness, the word you want is subconsciously.
  • Nothing special vs. Nothing specific: While 'nothing special' is grammatically correct, it usually means something was boring. If you mean 'I can't pinpoint one exact thing,' then phrases like 'nothing specific' or 'it's hard to put my finger on it' work perfectly.
  • As what I felt: A smoother way to compare feelings is to say 'the exact same feeling as 15 years ago' or 'the feeling I had...'

Key Vocabulary & Grammar Tips
  • Show is of around 4 seasons vs. Has about 4 seasons: In English, we don't usually say a show 'is of' seasons. Instead, say a show 'has [number] seasons' or 'ran for [number] seasons.'
  • End up in a night vs. Finish it in a night: 'End up' usually describes an unexpected final result. To talk about completing a task or a series, use 'finish it,' 'marathon it,' or 'binge it.'
  • Justy: This was just a minor typo for 'just.'
  • The other person vs. Someone else: 'The other person' sounds like there are only two specific people in the whole room. Since Peter can touch anyone to get their powers, 'someone else' fits better.
